UNDERWEIGHT

What is underweight?

If your BMI is less than 18.5 it falls within the underweight range.If your BMI is 18.5 to 24.9,it falls within the Healthy Weight range. That is being underweight means you are below the healthy weight range — the weight that research has shown you need to be if your body is to work properly. Being outside this range, whether overweight or underweight, can seriously affect your health.


What causes being underweight?

People become underweight for many different reasons. Some may be born naturally small and their low BMI is due to their genes. Some may have a very high metabolism and find it hard to put on weight, even if they eat foods that have a lot of calories.

Some people may not follow a healthy, balanced diet because they forget to eat or they cannot afford nutritious foods. Others may not be eating properly because they are sick, or their medicines make them feel nauseous. Some people who do a lot of physical activity burn up more calories than they can eat, leading to their being underweight.

A common belief is that healthy underweight individuals can ‘eat what they want’ and then burn it off either by high levels of activity or elevated metabolism


What are the problems?

If you are underweight, you may be at greater risk of certain health conditions, including malnutrition, osteoporosis, decreased muscle strength, hypothermia and lowered immunity. You are more likely to die at a younger age.

Disadvantages of Underweight

  • Malnutrition: Underweight people may not be getting enough essential nutrients from their diet, which can lead to malnutrition and related health problems.
  • Increased risk of osteoporosis: Low body weight is associated with a higher risk of osteoporosis, which is a condition that weakens bones and makes them more likely to fracture.
  • Reduced fertility: women may have irregular menstrual cycles or may stop menstruating altogether, which can make it difficult to conceive.
  • Slower healing: Underweight individuals may experience slower healing from injuries and illnesses.
  • Muscle loss: Being underweight can lead to muscle loss, which can result in weakness and decreased physical performance.
  • Psychological effects: Underweight individuals may experience negative psychological effects, such as poor body image, low self-esteem, and social isolation. It is important to maintain a healthy weight to avoid these potential health risks.

How to overcome this problem

If you are underweight, it is important to address the issue to maintain good health. Here are some tips to help you overcome the problem of being underweight:

  1. Eat a balanced diet: Consume a diet that includes all the essential nutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals. Include more nutrient-dense foods like whole grains, lean proteins, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds in your diet.
  2. Increase your calorie intake: If you are not consuming enough calories, your body won't be able to build muscle or maintain healthy body weight. Try to eat more frequent meals, and add calorie-dense foods to your diet such as peanut butter, avocados, and cheese.
  3. Engage in regular exercise: Exercise can help you build muscle mass and increase your appetite. Try doing strength training exercises like weightlifting, resistance bands, or bodyweight exercises to help build muscle.
  4. Avoid smoking and excessive drinking: Smoking and drinking can suppress your appetite and cause other health problems. It is best to avoid them.
  5. Manage stress: Stress can affect your appetite and cause weight loss. Try to manage stress with techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.
  6. Get enough sleep: Lack of sleep can affect your appetite and metabolism. Aim to get at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night.
  7. Consider seeking professional help: If you are having trouble gaining weight, consider consulting a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian to help create a personalized plan to meet your specific needs. Remember that gaining weight in a healthy way takes time and patience, so it's important to be consistent with these lifestyle changes.
